Purpose

There is a reason Clean Coastlines was designed. This is a school project to help spread awareness to students, that we need to keep our coastlines clean. 2019 was the year Auckland was considered by the Ministry for the Environment to have the most polluted waterways. 62 percent of rivers and lakes were graded poor, none graded as good. However, there is an organisation out there in New Zealand known as Sustainable Coastlines.

They are an organisation where they focus on cleaning our waters. Therefore, building this website was aimed to support their cause. It is meant to bring them the opportunity to clean our oceans, if we no longer want to see waters filled with rubbish. This is the case with Auckland's polluted waters.

Mission

In New Zealand, Sustainable Coastlines is an organisation that is a registered New Zealand charity. More importantly, they are on a mission to keep coastlines and waterways safe from pollution.

Therefore, they deliver coastal clean-up events, public awareness campaigns, educational programmes, and riparian planting projects. They work with local community groups, interns, volunteers, and ambassadors. Also, work with people to enable them, delivering those activities into their own communities.

Goals

Sustainable Coastlines love coasts and oceans, they want them litter free. They clean up beaches and deliver education to prevent litter, especially single-use plastics. Waters in New Zealand need our aid. As a result, they restore waterways by planting trees. Secondly, enable communities to scale-up local solutions. Finally, educate people on the issues concerning freshwater.

Many people created the challenges we face with the state of our oceans today. So it has become our responsibility to keep our coastlines sustainable. This organisation uses behaviour-focused education, inspiring events and training to encourage people to take care of the places they love. Sustainable Coastlines provide three programmes to achieve their goals.

Litter Intelligence

Litter Intelligence is their newest programme, launched on May 2018. This programme involves collecting litter data, inspires widespread action for solutions, and powerful insights about the problem. It all comes down to improving decision making to solve our litter problem.

Love Your Coast

Beach clean ups have been important to them, from the beginning. Plastic pollution however, is not solved by easily collecting it where it ends up. As a result, this program will provide education into the equation, delivering positive change in the everyday lives of people.

Love Your Water

Keeping waterways free of litter is the main aspect of keeping waters sustainable. Yet it is not the only solution. This program enables practical action - tree planting - and education on pollution in our inland lakes and rivers. It all is meant to make sure the health of our waterways before they flow to the sea.

Keeping waters sustainable

1. Conserve water, so excess runoff and wastewater will not flow into the ocean.

2. Reduce pollutants by disposing herbicides, pesticides, and cleaning products properly.

3. Reduce waste by cutting down what you throw away.

4. Shop wisely. You can do this by bringing a reusable bag and choose sustainable seafood.

5. Use fuel efficient vehicles, or ride a bike. That is how you reduce vehicle pollution.

6. Use less energy so it does not overset your thermostat.

7. Follow "catch and release" practices, keeping more fish alive.

8. Anchor in sandy areas far from coral and sea grasses.

9. Survival and healthy habitat go hand in hand. Must treat with care.

10. Volunteer for cleanups hosted by Sustainable Coastlines.
